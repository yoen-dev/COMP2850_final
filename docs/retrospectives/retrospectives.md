# Sprint Retrospectives
**NourishWell — Good Food & Healthy Eating — COMP2850**

---

## Sprint 1 Retrospective

**Date:** 22 March 2025
**Location:** Online (Microsoft Teams)
**Attendees:** Che Lin, Tengchuan Jiang, Chin Pang Chan, Baiyi He
**Facilitated by:** Che Lin

---

### What went well

- Che Lin completed the first draft of Personas on 25 February and finalised the confirmed version incorporating team feedback on 7 March
- Che Lin published a development plan and task scoring breakdown to the team group on 4 March, giving the team a clear picture of priorities and expectations
- At the 11 March meeting the team discussed the frontend design direction together; Che Lin prepared a design proposal drawing on several reference websites, which the team reviewed and approved; Che Lin shared the finalised proposal with the group after the meeting
- Che Lin published a GitHub collaboration guide on 19 March, establishing shared conventions for version control
- Che Lin updated the project development plan on 22 March to reflect the latest progress
- The GitHub repository was set up and the basic workflow of feature branches and pull requests was established

### What didn't go well

- The team group was created on 13 February but the frontend direction remained unresolved until mid-March, leaving nearly four weeks without substantial progress
- Although the team had approved Che Lin's design direction at the 11 March meeting, Tengchuan Jiang subsequently produced a separate design proposal that diverged significantly from the agreed direction, creating a conflict that required an additional meeting to resolve
- Because the frontend direction was unresolved for an extended period, the backend team was also unable to make meaningful progress, creating a dependency chain that stalled overall development
- Team communication was not proactive enough — messages were slow to receive replies and task progress lacked transparency
- Che Lin should have been more assertive in driving team progress earlier; much of the coordination work was self-initiated rather than distributed across the team

### What to change — Sprint 2 actions

- **Che Lin to take full ownership of frontend:** Given the extended delay in frontend progress, Che Lin decided to take on all frontend design and development independently
- **Improve communication frequency:** The team agreed to share task updates more proactively in the group chat rather than waiting for scheduled meetings
- **Backend to begin development:** With the frontend direction now confirmed, the backend team should begin actual feature development; Che Lin will provide a clear integration document as a development reference
- **Che Lin to follow up on progress more actively:** As the coordination lead, Che Lin will take a more proactive role in checking in on each member's tasks

---

### What went well

- Che Lin completed the homepage and login page frontend on 24 March and uploaded them to GitHub, alongside a frontend–backend integration guide for those pages; the backend team subsequently completed the backend implementation for this section
- Che Lin completed all remaining NourishWell frontend pages by 16 April, including the landing page, subscriber Dashboard (food diary, recipes, favourites, recipe comparison, meal planner), Pro Dashboard (client management, messaging, health analytics, appointment management), and the help centre pages
- After completing the full frontend, Che Lin produced a comprehensive backend integration document detailing the required API endpoints and data formats for all remaining features, published to the team group; the backend team confirmed they would proceed based on the document
- User Stories were updated through to v2 and v3, continuously reflecting the evolving scope of the project

### What didn't go well

- Overall backend progress remained behind schedule; frontend–backend integration has not yet been achieved and the frontend currently runs as a standalone static application
- While team communication improved, proactive progress updates continued to rely mainly on Che Lin
- The frontend was completed entirely by Che Lin alone, which diverged from the original plan of two members sharing frontend responsibilities

### What to change — final sprint actions

- **Backend to prioritise core API development:** The backend team should focus on completing the food diary, nutrition calculation, and other core endpoints based on Che Lin's integration document
- **Schedule a frontend–backend integration session:** A specific date for integration work should be agreed to ensure a functional connected version is available before the submission deadline
- **Arrange UX testing as soon as possible:** Che Lin will organise user testing sessions, collect and record feedback, and use the findings to support the documentation evidence chain
- **Complete remaining Wiki documentation:** Che Lin will continue to finalise outstanding documents including the security strategy, Git conventions, and accessibility audit report
